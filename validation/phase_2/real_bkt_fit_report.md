## Why the plausible-band gate failed

Only 12.7% of skills (20 / 157) land in the spec-defined bands
`p_slip ∈ [0.02, 0.20]`, `p_guess ∈ [0.10, 0.35]`,
`p_transit ∈ [0.05, 0.40]`. Reported honestly per the Phase 2
non-negotiable (do not silently clip; do not silently reframe).

### Structural diagnosis

- **Median attempts per user per skill: 1.97.** BKT's EM needs
  multi-attempt per-student sequences to identify the hidden-state
  transition probability `p_transit`. With a median of two attempts
  per user per skill, EM cannot distinguish "learned via transition"
  from "guessed correctly" with any reliability.
- **Bound pinning:** across the EM identifiability bounds
  `(slip ∈ [0.01, 0.3], guess ∈ [0.01, 0.4], transit ∈ [0.01, 0.5])`,
  fits pin at the upper or lower bound for **27–42%** of skills per
  parameter. Bound-pinning is the signature of EM failing to recover
  interior optima — here a direct consequence of short sequences, not
  optimiser tolerance.
- **Skills with median attempts/user ≥ 10:** only 4 out of 157. Even
  on this subset, only 25% land in-band. The data does not carry the
  BKT signal at population scale.

### Why the 2012-2013 release differs from the canonical BKT corpus

Classic BKT calibration (Corbett & Anderson 1995; Beck & Chang 2007)
uses the **2009-2010 ASSISTments Skill Builder** release, whose
per-skill *mastery tutors* require students to practice a skill to a
streak criterion before advancing — producing 5–30+ attempt sequences
per (user, skill). The **2012-2013 release** used here is a mixed
assessment / prediction corpus whose per-skill sequences are much
shorter.

This matches the Phase 2 plan's Q1 concern (`docs/simulator/phase_2_plan.md §2.2`):
the 2012-2013 CSV is not a drop-in substitute for the 2009-2010
Skill Builder for BKT purposes.

### Proposed remediation (pick one)

1. **Ship the 2009-2010 Skill Builder CSV into S3** and re-run
   `run_real.py`. No code change required; the loader is already
   compatible. _Preferred_ — it's the dataset BKT is designed for.
2. **Accept the weaker BKT fits for Gate A** and continue with the
   current parquet. The downstream simulator uses BKT for mastery
   threshold checks; imperfect per-skill params still yield a usable
   simulator, though PR A2's BKT recovery target (±0.05 on ≥80% of
   skills) will be harder to meet.
3. **Augment with Bayesian BKT** (Pardos 2011, informative priors on
   slip/guess) in a follow-up PR. Scope creep — push to Phase 3.

PR A1 is opened as **draft** with (1) recommended. PR A2 will be
blocked on the remediation choice being made.
